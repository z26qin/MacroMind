How to read the Regime verdict

Each country in the Regime tab gets one verdict. It is decided by a fixed ladder of rules evaluated top to bottom — the first rule that matches wins. Three numbers feed it.

regime_score — fundamentals
Weighted blend of five structural buckets (policy, liquidity, foreign access, rating momentum, index catalyst), clipped to [−1, +1]. How strong the underlying setup is.
narrative_gap — the opportunity
regime_score minus what's already priced in (the consensus narrative). How far the hard setup runs ahead of the market story — the size of the edge.
confirmation — corroboration
Average of seven cross-asset channels (equity, sovereign credit, FX, CDS, ETF flows, local banks, rates). Whether markets are actually backing the call.
The verdict ladder
Repricing / Early — act now Unconfirmed — setup good, markets not corroborating Deteriorating — fundamentals breaking down Priced in / Neutral — no edge

Two asymmetries worth remembering. Deteriorating is checked first, so weak fundamentals win even over a large gap. And Priced in / Neutral are never gated — confirmation only matters when the verdict is telling you to act (Early / Repricing). All thresholds live in regime_config.yaml.
